Surface mount technology (SMT) assembly

Fine-pitch, double-sided SMT — down to 01005 passives and 0.2 mm micro-BGA pitch — with solder-paste inspection, 3D AOI and X-ray built into every board, not just automotive jobs. The same qualified process runs across our plants in China, Thailand, Cambodia and the USA.

Capability overview

Three things an engineering buyer evaluates on an SMT line — the process, the component envelope, and how every board is inspected.

[ Board exiting a multi-zone reflow oven ]
Process

A complete SMT line from solder-paste print through inspection, with vacuum reflow and selective soldering where the build needs it.

  • ›Print → SPI → place → reflow → AOI → X-ray → ICT → de-panel
  • ›Convection reflow in air or nitrogen; vacuum N₂ reflow for void reduction
  • ›Selective wave & robotic soldering for through-hole
  • ›De-panel by routing, laser cutting, V-cut or punching

Why run SMT with Hana

SMT is qualified across China, Thailand, Cambodia and the USA, so a program can shift sites for capacity or resilience as a documented first-article qualification — not a fresh introduction.

EMS + OSAT, vertically integrated

Frequently asked questions

What is the smallest component Hana can place on an SMT line?+
Down to 01005 passives, with micro-BGA and WLCSP attachment to 0.2 mm pitch and QFP to 0.4 mm pitch.
Does Hana inspect every board, or only high-reliability work?+
Solder-paste inspection, 3D AOI and X-ray run on the line by default, with full material, unit and product traceability — not only on automotive or medical jobs.
Can an SMT program transfer between Hana sites?+
Yes — SMT is qualified across China, Thailand, Cambodia and the USA, so a build can move between sites as a documented first-article qualification rather than a full re-NPI.
What board and panel sizes can Hana handle?+
PCB panels up to 440 × 600 mm, with convection (air or nitrogen) and vacuum nitrogen reflow.
What quality and certification systems cover SMT?+
Customer IP is protected under ISO 27001, and Hana runs dedicated medical and automotive assembly lines in a fully ESD-controlled facility.
