ABOUT

Why Hana Group

Hana brings semiconductor assembly and test together with full electronics manufacturing — OSAT and EMS in one independent, publicly listed company, across five countries. Specialized in microelectronics since 1978.

Contact us Explore capabilities
Vision

To be the electronics manufacturing services partner of choice — delivering high-quality microelectronics manufacturing across a broad field of related disciplines.

Mission

To build long-term value for shareholders, customers, and employees — through strong customer satisfaction, the wellbeing of our people, responsible environmental and social practices, and a culture of innovation.

THE HANA ADVANTAGE

Vertically Integrated Operations

Many manufacturers can do one or the other but Hana does both. OSAT — outsourced semiconductor assembly and test — packages and tests the chips. EMS — electronics manufacturing services — builds the circuit boards and finished products around them. With both inside one company, a product that needs chip-level packaging and board-level assembly can move through both without leaving Hana.

For a buyer, that means fewer suppliers to qualify and manage, design and intellectual property kept within a single organization, and closer control over quality, cost, and schedule.

Geographic resilience

Manufacturing across five countries

Our global manufacturing footprint within Hana gives customers opportunities for program to be dual-sourced across borders without changing suppliers, closer to final markets and adaptable to different wage and technological requirements between countries.

Hana gives customers room to manage regional risk — tariffs, demand swings, and supply disruption — including a domestic option in the United States and TAA compliant manufacturing in Cambodia.

Track record

Building microelectronics since 1978

Hana started in Bangkok in 1978 and has been listed on the Stock Exchange of Thailand since 1993. It is independent — not a captive plant owned by a single OEM — which means stability, transparency, and decisions made for the long term.

Many customer relationships run more than twenty years. In contract manufacturing, that continuity is the clearest signal that the work holds up.
