Automotive · Sub-market

Power modules for electrified vehicles

Power modules are the assemblies that switch high current in a vehicle's powertrain — inside traction inverters, on-board chargers and DC-DC converters. Hana assembles IGBT and silicon-carbide modules on automotive-qualified lines, built around the thermal path that keeps them reliable.

SiC runs hotter and switches faster than silicon. A sintered die-attach joint holds up to higher temperatures and thermal cycling than standard solder, so the module survives the powertrain’s duty cycle.

Through DBC or AMB ceramic substrates, baseplate attach, and void-controlled solder joints verified by X-ray — the heat path from die to coolant is designed and inspected, not assumed.
